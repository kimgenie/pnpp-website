The PNP Partners mark is a single brass dot — the persimmon (감), a Korean symbol of patience and ripening. The dot does one job: be the dot. Over time, it becomes the firm.

The mark

A perfect circle in brass. No stem, no leaf, no ornament. Confidence by subtraction.

Construction & clear space

Every measurement is expressed in units of x — the diameter of the dot. This lets the system scale from favicon to billboard without losing proportion.

Clear space. Reserve a margin of at least 0.75x on all sides of the mark. Nothing crosses this boundary — not text, not images, not other logos.

Minimum size. The mark must never appear smaller than 16px digital / 5mm print. Below that, edge anti-aliasing destroys the form.

Wordmark spacing. In horizontal lockup, the gap between dot and "P" is 0.5x. Cap-height of "P" equals x.

Alignment. Dot center aligns to the optical center of the wordmark — not the cap line, not the baseline.

Don't

The mark earns its power through restraint. Anything below breaks it.

Don't stretch or squish
Don't recolor outside the system
Don't add shadows or bevels
Don't use as outline only
Don't apply gradients
Don't redraw or distort the shape

Voice & tone

Speak like a senior consultant who has been in the room and seen the failure mode. Direct, considered, sparing.

Do

Say less. Land harder.

Use specifics: numbers, durations, named firms.

Frame outcomes the CEO actually loses sleep over.

Write the way you would brief a board.

Treat Korean and English as equals, not translations.

Don't

"Holistic," "synergy," "tailored solutions."

Hedged claims. Marketing bombast.

Three adjectives where one verb would do.

Promise outcomes you can't measure.

Translate Korean copy. Write it.
